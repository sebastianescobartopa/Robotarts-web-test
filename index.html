Abstract

This paper explores the co-creation of visual artworks generated through the interaction between a differential robot, its environment, and a person’s brain waves. The traces produced by the robot’s movement and a person’s brain waves captured by an electroencephalographic (EEG) device are combined to generate shapes with artistic visual potential. Introduction

This work draws inspiration from opportunities at the intersection of BRI and painting to propose an interactive system that translates EEG signals into visual artworks. Using this system in a series of experiments presented in a gallery format, each resulting piece represented the visual outcome derived from the EEG and robot movement. We also describe the approach used for the generation of visual expressions from brain waves and robot movements
